Frequently Asked Questions

It's recommended to perform routine plumbing maintenance at least once a year to ensure your plumbing system is functioning effectively and to catch any potential issues early.

Common signs include unusual noises in pipes, slow draining sinks, low water pressure, and higher than usual water bills. These could indicate leaks or blockages that need attention.

To prevent clogs, avoid disposing of grease, hair, and large food particles down drains. Use drain guards to catch debris and regularly clean them to prevent buildup.

Check for leaks, corrosion, and cracks in pipes. Ensure all faucets are operating correctly, inspect water heaters for signs of wear, and test water pressure throughout the home.

To prevent pipes from freezing, insulate them with foam sleeves, keep cabinet doors open to let warm air circulate, and let taps drip slightly during extremely cold weather.
