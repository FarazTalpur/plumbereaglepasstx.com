Frequently Asked Questions

To unclog a bathroom sink drain, start by removing any visible debris or hair from the drain. Then, use a plunger to dislodge the clog. If that doesn't work, try using a mixture of baking soda and vinegar. Pour half a cup of baking soda followed by half a cup of vinegar into the drain, let it sit for about 15 minutes, and then rinse with hot water. For stubborn clogs, a drain snake can be used to reach deeper blockages.

A constantly running toilet is often due to a faulty flapper valve or an improperly adjusted float in the tank. Check to see if the flapper is sealing properly over the flush valve seat. If not, it may need to be replaced. Adjusting the float to ensure the water level is below the overflow tube can stop the toilet from running. Also, inspect the fill valve for leaks or damage.

Low water pressure in a shower can be caused by a clogged showerhead or issues with the plumbing. Start by unscrewing the showerhead and soaking it in vinegar overnight to remove mineral deposits. If the issue persists, check for leaks in the plumbing or consult a plumber to inspect the pressure regulators or pipes for any blockages.

To prevent bathroom pipes from freezing, insulate exposed pipes with foam pipe insulation or heat tape. Keep your bathroom warm during cold weather by allowing warm air to circulate around the pipes. You can also let faucets drip slowly to prevent freezing by relieving pressure in the water system. If you have outdoor faucets, make sure they are turned off and drain the hoses.

A leaky faucet is typically caused by worn-out washers, gaskets, or seals. Start by turning off the water supply and disassembling the faucet. Inspect the parts for wear and replace any that appear damaged. Common fixes include replacing the O-ring or washer, or in the case of a cartridge-type faucet, replacing the entire cartridge. Reassemble the faucet and turn the water back on to check for leaks.
